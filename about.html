About Campus Convoz


Campus Convoz is a project by Sarah Dapul-Weberman and Scott Khamphoune for CS376: Research Topics in Human-Computer Interaction, taught by Michael Bernstein in Spring 2013. Right now, Campus Convoz has only launched to Stanford University.

Campus Convoz was inspired by our obsession with Stanford Confessions and Omegle.

Stanford Confessions allows students to anonymously express themselves, but doesn't have a way for students to engage with one another anonymously in a one-on-one conersation.
Omegle provides for that anonymous conversation, but has no attachment to a community whatsoever and thus loses the meaningfulness of a community coming together and talking to each other.

We are interested in the way anonymity acts as a double-edged sword: on one hand, it protects our identity and offers a safe way to express ourselves genuinely, but on the other hand, it may encourage 'trolls' to act irresponsibly and offensively. Our hope is that Campus Convoz can become a safe, outlet where students can converse with each other intimately.


Why "Campus Convoz"?
Well, campusconvos.com was taken. So was campusconversations.com, campuschat.com, campusanonymous.com, and basically everything else we tried.
